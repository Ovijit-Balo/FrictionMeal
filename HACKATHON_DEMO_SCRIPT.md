# FrictionMeal Hackathon Demo Script (3-4 Minutes)

## Demo Title
FrictionMeal: Turning Friction Into Better Decisions

## Target Duration
3 minutes 30 seconds

## Demo Format
Narration + screen recording + face-cam intro/outro

---

## Script With Video Steps

### 1) 0:00-0:20 | Hook (Face-Cam)
**Narration**

One word. Infinite interpretations.  
Friction.  
For years, we optimized everything for speed: instant food, instant checkout, instant regret.  
FrictionMeal takes the opposite stance: the right friction at the right moment can protect health and budget.

**Video**

- Face-cam, confident intro
- Show project name card: **FrictionMeal**

---

### 2) 0:20-0:45 | Theme + Position (Slide or Landing View)
**Narration**

Our hackathon challenge says: do not remove friction, understand it.  
So our position is simple: when someone is about to make an expensive or unhealthy food decision, the app creates a smart pause, not a hard block.  
That moment of reflection changes outcomes.

**Video**

- Show the hackathon theme statement briefly
- Switch to app home/dashboard

---

### 3) 0:45-1:20 | Problem Framing (Dashboard)
**Narration**

Most nutrition apps only track after the decision is made.  
FrictionMeal intervenes before the decision is finalized.  
It combines meal quality signals, daily budget signals, and behavior context to detect risky choices in real time.

**Video**

- Show daily summary
- Highlight budget indicator and meal score area
- Point to current day progress

---

### 4) 1:20-2:10 | Core Flow: Log Meal + Smart Pause
**Narration**

Let me log a meal now.  
I enter a premium but risky meal combination.  
The system evaluates nutrition quality, spending impact, and whether I am already off-track today.  
Now you can see the Smart Pause: it explains why this choice may be risky and gives alternatives.  
Notice: this is not punishment. It is guided friction.

**Video**

- Open meal logger
- Add meal items and price
- Submit meal
- Show Smart Pause modal with rationale and suggestions
- Hover on key warning lines and recommended alternatives

---

### 5) 2:10-2:45 | Alternatives + Decision Ownership
**Narration**

This is where our interpretation of friction matters.  
We do not force a yes or no.  
We offer better options with clear trade-offs: lower cost, better nutrition, or both.  
The user keeps control, but now with better context.

**Video**

- Click through suggested alternatives
- Show improved option
- Show updated score/cost effect

---

### 6) 2:45-3:10 | Logs and Weekly Insight
**Narration**

Over time, these micro-pauses become behavior change.  
In the log and summary views, users can see patterns: where money leaks, where nutrition slips, and where improvements happen.

**Video**

- Open meal history view
- Open summary view
- Show weekly trend chart and score progression

---

### 7) 3:10-3:30 | Closing (Face-Cam + App Split View)
**Narration**

Our mission can be written like this: interpret friction, take a stance, ship something real.  
FrictionMeal proves friction is not the enemy.  
Mindless friction is bad. Purposeful friction is powerful.  
That is our build. That is our stance.

**Video**

- Face-cam closing with app visible in split view
- End on project logo/title

---

## One-Take Recording Checklist

1. Start with camera on and high energy.
2. Keep cursor movement slow and intentional while explaining.
3. Zoom in when Smart Pause appears.
4. Spend most time on the core flow, not setup.
5. End with your stance statement, not a feature list.

---

## Hackathon Theme Mapping (Quick Judge Summary)

- **Theme phrase:** One word. Infinite interpretations.
- **Your interpretation of friction:** Cognitive friction at high-risk decision moments.
- **Your position:** Do not remove friction. Add purposeful friction.
- **Implementation:** Smart Pause + contextual alternatives + user control.
- **Real-world impact:** Better spending choices, healthier behavior, less regret.
